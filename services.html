Property Documentation

When purchasing a home, the buyer should always receive a deed, which is a legal document that transfers ownership of the property from the seller to the buyer. The deed should be recorded with the local government, typically with the county clerk, and will serve as proof of ownership. The deed should include the name of the buyer, the seller, and a description of the property, including the address. Other documents that may be included with the purchase of a home include a title report, surveys, and other legal documents such as closing documents.

Property Registration Buying and Selling

Property registration is the process of registering the sale or purchase of a property with the relevant authorities. This process is necessary for legal proof of ownership, and to ensure that all legal documents are in order. The registration process also helps to protect the interests of both the buyer and the seller.


The process of registering a property usually involves completing a registration form, paying a registration fee, and providing relevant documentation. The form will usually require information such as the name and address of the buyer, the seller, and any other parties involved in the transaction. The buyer and seller must also provide proof that they are legally allowed to buy and sell the property, such as a deed or title.


Once the registration form has been completed, the property must be inspected by a qualified surveyor. This surveyor will check that the property meets all legal requirements, such as zoning laws and building codes. Then, the property must be registered with the relevant authorities. This may include the local municipality, the state land registry, or the national land registry.


The registration process can vary depending on the type of property being purchased. For example, residential properties require a different set of documents than commercial properties. Additionally, the registration process may take longer in certain jurisdictions.

Wet Land Convertion (Nanjai)

Wetland conversion is the process of converting wetland habitats into land for agricultural or other uses. In Nanjai, this process has been used to convert wetlands into rice paddies, ponds, and other agricultural uses. This has resulted in the loss of valuable habitat for wildlife, increased soil erosion, and other environmental impacts. To mitigate these impacts, Nanjai has implemented a number of measures, including the establishment of buffer zones, water management systems, and other conservation measures. Additionally, they have worked to restore degraded wetlands by planting native vegetation, controlling weeds, and providing habitat for native species. These efforts have helped improve the health of local wetland ecosystems, and have allowed for the sustainable use of the land.

All NOC related Land from Public Works Department (PWD)

The Public Works Department (PWD) is responsible for the acquisition of land for public works projects such as roads, bridges, and other infrastructure. The PWD may acquire land through direct purchase, exchange, or condemnation. The PWD may also acquire land through a process known as a Non-Occupancy Certificate (NOC). The NOC is a certificate granted by the local government that authorizes the transfer of ownership of unoccupied land from one individual to another. The NOC is typically used to transfer land from a private owner to the government for public use.

Intellectual Property Rights ( Trademarks, copyright and patent)

Intellectual property rights are legal rights that protect the intellectual property (IP) of an individual or business. Types of intellectual property rights include trademarks, copyrights, and patents.


Trademarks: A trademark is a sign, symbol, or phrase that is used to distinguish a product or service from that of another company. It can be a word, phrase, symbol, design, sound, smell, or combination of any of these elements. Trademarks are used to protect brands and ensure that customers can identify and purchase the products or services that they are looking for.


Copyright: A copyright is a legal right that grants exclusive rights to creators for their original works of authorship. It gives creators the exclusive right to reproduce, distribute, display, and perform their work, as well as to make derivative works. Copyright protection does not extend to ideas, concepts, or facts.


Patents: A patent is a legal right granted by the government that gives an inventor exclusive rights to make, use, and sell an invention for a certain period of time. Patents are used to protect inventions from being copied or used by others without permission.

Property litigation Solutions

Property litigation solutions may involve various strategies and tactics to resolve disputes involving real estate. Some of the most common solutions include negotiation, arbitration, mediation, and litigation.


Negotiation: Negotiation is a process in which parties attempt to reach a mutually beneficial agreement without having to resort to the courts. It involves mutual discussion and compromise to come up with a solution.


Arbitration: Arbitration is a dispute resolution process in which a neutral third party, called an arbitrator, renders a decision after reviewing the evidence presented by both parties. The arbitrator's decision is binding and may be enforceable in court.


Mediation: Mediation is a process in which a neutral third party, called a mediator, helps the parties reach a mutually agreeable resolution of their dispute. Mediation is often used to resolve disputes over the ownership or use of real estate.


Litigation: Litigation is the process of taking a dispute to court. When a dispute is litigated, each party presents their case to a judge or jury, who makes a decision based on the evidence presented. The decision of the court is final and binding.
